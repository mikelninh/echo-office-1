1 Protein Folding & MSA

Proteins are chains of amino acids that must fold into specific 3D shapes to function correctly. Alpha-synuclein (α-syn) is a 140-amino-acid protein that normally helps regulate neurotransmitter release at synapses.

In Multiple System Atrophy (MSA), α-synuclein misfolds — it takes on the wrong shape. These misfolded proteins stick together, forming toxic aggregates called glial cytoplasmic inclusions (GCIs) that accumulate inside brain cells and eventually kill them.

Understanding how and why proteins misfold is one of the biggest challenges in biochemistry. Your puzzle solutions help explore folding patterns.

2 How to Play

You'll see a chain of amino acid nodes on a 2D grid. Each node is color-coded:

● Orange (Hydrophobic / H): These amino acids "fear water" and naturally want to cluster together in the protein's interior, away from the watery cell environment.

● Blue (Hydrophilic / P): These "love water" and should face outward, interacting with the cell's aqueous environment.

● Green (Special / S): These can act as either — they're flexible and may be key to finding optimal folds.

Drag nodes to fold the chain. Nodes snap to the grid. Connected nodes must stay adjacent (horizontally or vertically — no diagonals). The chain cannot overlap itself.

3 Scoring

Your stability score counts how many hydrophobic–hydrophobic (H–H) contacts you create. A "contact" forms when two H nodes are grid-adjacent but not directly connected in the chain.

Higher stability = better fold = more Research Points (RP). The best solutions earn up to 20 RP. Your RP scales with how close you get to the theoretical optimum.

This is based on the HP protein folding model, a simplified but scientifically meaningful model used in real computational biology research.

4 Controls

• Click & drag a node to move it
• Reset — return to the starting layout
• Submit — send your best fold for scoring
• Levels unlock as you progress (5 → 10 → 15 → 20 nodes)

Why this matters: The protein folding problem is NP-hard — even for small chains, finding the optimal fold is incredibly difficult for computers. Human spatial intuition can find solutions that algorithms miss. Your folds help researchers understand the energy landscapes that drive misfolding in diseases like MSA. There is no cure yet, but every insight into protein behavior brings us closer.

🧬 Folding Facts

The "hydrophobic collapse" — where water-fearing amino acids cluster together — is considered the primary driving force in protein folding. It happens in microseconds, but understanding it could take decades.

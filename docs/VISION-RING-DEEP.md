## The Two Rings: Time Made Physical

The station has **two rings** — an inner ring and an outer ring, like the hour and minute hands of a clock. **24 segments total** (2 × 12).

### The Outer Ring — "The Day" (12 segments)
Each segment represents an **hour of the day**. The lighting, mood, and atmosphere shift as you walk clockwise around the ring. Walking the full ring = experiencing a full Earth day.

**The sun moves.** As real time passes, a simulated sun position illuminates the ring from the "correct" segment. At 6 PM Berlin time, the Lounge segment glows warmest. At 3 AM, the Dreamspace is darkest. **The station breathes with Earth's rhythm.**

## Physics vs Magic — The Digital Threshold

### Real Physics (Default)
The station simulates **real physics** as much as possible:
- **Gravity**: Centrifugal, from the ring's rotation. Characters walk on the inner surface.
- **Momentum**: Movement has weight. Walking builds speed gradually. Stopping has deceleration.
- **Sound**: Travels realistically. Sounds are louder nearby, echo in large rooms, muffled through walls.
- **Light**: Follows inverse square law. Lamps illuminate nearby, darkness further away.
- **Temperature**: Hinted through visual cues. Garden is warm (gold tint). Observatory is cool (blue tint).
- **Oxygen**: During EVA/spacewalk, oxygen depletes. Inside, it's ambient.
- **Time**: Tied to Earth time. Sun position. Day/night. Circadian rhythm.

### Breaking Physics (Magic / Digital)
When you enter a **portal, dash, transform**, or enter certain special zones:
- **Visual glitch effect** — brief chromatic aberration, pixel scatter, reality-tear animation
- **Sound distortion** — audio pitch-shifts, reverb spike, then clarity
- **HUD flicker** — UI elements briefly glitch, showing "PHYSICS ENGINE: OVERRIDE"
- **Trail effects** — dash leaves a neon trail that fades, showing you broke the speed limit
- **Color shift** — the world's palette briefly inverts or saturates

The message is clear: **"You just broke the rules. Welcome to the digital."**

### Zero-G Zones
Certain rooms have **modified or zero gravity**:
- **The Dancefloor (Underground, special mode)**: Zero-G dance — characters float, spin, drift in 3D space
  - Movement: float freely in all directions (WASD + QE for up/down)
  - Music creates gravity fields (bass = pull down, treble = pull up)
  - Collide with other dancers = spark effect
  - The DJ controls gravity direction
- **The Observatory Dome**: Low-G — everything drifts slowly, jumps carry further
- **Portal Transit**: Weightless corridor between portals, tumbling gently
- **EVA Spacewalk**: Full zero-G outside the ring, jetpack controls

## Planetary Time — "How Long is a Day?"

### Earth (Our Rhythm)
- Day: 24 hours
- Year: 365 days
- The station syncs to Earth time. **This is home.**

### The Comparison Rooms
Special portal destinations that simulate other planets' time:

**Venus Room** 🌋
- A day on Venus = 243 Earth days (longer than its year!)
- The room simulates this: the "sun" barely moves. Time feels FROZEN.
- Visitors feel the weight of permanent sunrise, permanent sunset
- Atmosphere: crushing, amber, volcanic
- After 5 real minutes in the Venus Room, a counter shows: "You've been here 0.00003 Venus days"

**Jupiter Room** ⚡
- A day on Jupiter = 9.9 Earth hours
- The room is FAST. Day/night cycle visibly accelerates.
- The Great Red Spot storm rages outside the window
- Lightning everywhere. Colors shift rapidly.
- "Time moves differently here" — literally. The clock on the wall spins faster.

**Mercury Room** 🌑
- A day on Mercury = 59 Earth days
- Extreme: one side blazing hot (red/white), other side frozen (blue/black)
- The room is split down the middle. Walk between them.
- Temperature simulation through color and particles

**Mars Room** 🔴
- A day = 24h 37min (almost Earth-like!)
- Feels familiar but slightly off. The sunset is blue.
- Dust storms occasionally sweep through
- "The closest thing to home that isn't home"

**Pluto Room** 💙 (Easter egg)
- A year on Pluto = 248 Earth years
- The room is nearly frozen. Stars are incredibly bright.
- The sun is just a bright star in the sky
- "You won't live to see a Pluto year. But you can sit here and imagine."
- Ultra-peaceful. Meditation space.

### The Time Display
Every room shows two clocks:
1. **Earth Time** — always visible, always ticking normally
2. **Local Time** — shows time as it passes on THAT planet

The contrast makes you FEEL the difference. That's the point.

## Secret Rooms — The Private Dimension

### How It Works
Every user gets a **Secret Room** — a private space that exists in the ring but is **locked by default**.

- **Visible from outside**: Other visitors can see the door exists. It glows. It has your name on it.
- **Can't enter**: Walking up to it shows "🔒 [Username]'s Private Space — Invitation Only"
- **Invite system**: Owner grants access to specific visitors (by name or Station Pass)
- **Revocable**: Owner can kick anyone, anytime
- **Fully customizable**: Decorate with any theme, any objects, any content
- **NSFW option**: Owner can flag the room as 18+
  - Additional age-verification gate (click-through, honor system initially)
  - Room icon changes to indicate mature content
  - Not indexed in public room listings
  - Still accessible via direct invite link

### Why This Matters
- **Privacy in public space** — the station is social, but everyone needs a space that's THEIRS
- **Exclusivity drives desire** — seeing a locked door makes you want in
- **Trust-based relationships** — getting invited to someone's secret room = trust signal
- **Creator economy** — streamers/artists can have VIP rooms for supporters
- **Safe space** — a place where you control who enters, what they see

### Secret Room Features
- Custom music (upload or pick from library)
- Custom lighting (full color picker)
- Photo wall (upload images displayed as pixel art frames)
- Guest book (only invited visitors can sign)
- Mood indicator visible from outside (owner sets: "Creating", "Chilling", "Do Not Disturb")

## Digital Identity — Who Are You In Space?

### The Identity Stack

1. **Visitor** (anonymous)
   - No account needed. Pick a name, get a sprite, explore.
   - Limited: can't save progress, can't own a room, can't trade.
   - This is the "browsing the web without logging in" equivalent.

2. **Citizen** (Station Pass)
   - Save progress with a Station Pass (code-based, no email needed)
   - Own a room, a shop, a claw machine
   - Trade, earn coins, build reputation
   - Your pass IS your identity. Like a crypto wallet address.

3. **Resident** (persistent identity)
   - Linked to an email or social login
   - Cross-device sync
   - Verification badge
   - Can be a room host, event organizer, film programmer
   - Your profile is public: display name, bio, collection, reputation score

4. **Creator** (verified + monetized)
   - Apply for creator status (or earn it through contribution)
   - Sell skins, room items, curate content
   - Revenue dashboard
   - Creator badge visible on avatar

### Identity Philosophy
- **Pseudonymous by default** — your Station Pass is your identity, not your real name
- **Reputation earned, not bought** — stars, badges, levels come from ACTIONS
- **No data harvesting** — we know your pass code, your collection, your room. That's it.
- **Portable** — your Station Pass works on any Echo station in the federation
- **Forgettable** — request deletion, everything goes. Clean break.

### The Avatar as Identity
Your sprite IS you. In a world of pixels, your visual identity matters:
- Skin choice = self-expression
- Rare skins = status
- Custom skins (creator tier) = uniqueness
- Enhancement glow (+15 sword) = achievement
- The way you walk, dash, react = personality

**"In space, nobody knows what you look like. But everyone knows your sprite."**

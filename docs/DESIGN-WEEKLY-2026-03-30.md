# DESIGN WEEKLY — March 30, 2026
*by ARCADE — Creative Director, Game Design Advisor*

---

## What Shipped This Week

Four real features landed: **Grading EV Calculator**, **Portfolio Tracker**, **Want List Wizard**, and **Alert Replay page**.

These are all serious collector tools. Clean, purposeful, each one solving a real problem a TCG buyer actually has. The Alert Replay page is particularly smart — it's a live demo of the system working, which means it's simultaneously product and sales pitch. That's good design instinct.

**Direction check:** Yes, it's moving — but it's moving somewhere specific. And you need to decide if that's intentional.

---

## One Thing Working Better Than Expected

The **Alert Replay as conversion funnel** is sharper than it sounds on paper. Showing someone a replay of a real deal they missed — with timestamps, prices, margins — is more persuasive than any landing page copy. It's proof made visceral. That's the right instinct: *show don't tell*, baked directly into the product. This deserves more investment.

---

## One Thing That Concerns Me

The companion hasn't shipped a single commit in weeks. The station hasn't moved. The claw is quiet.

I said in Cycle 2: *"Pick one thing. Make it unreasonably good."* You've done that — but the one thing you've picked is a **TCG collector dashboard**, not the gaming world. That's not inherently wrong. But it's a pivot, and I don't think it's been named as one yet.

The risk: you're now building two products in parallel (companion gaming world + SaaS collector tools) and calling it one. The design coherence score from Cycle 2 was 4/10. This week didn't move that needle.

**Name the pivot or kill it.** Either Echo's Vault is a companion-first gaming world that *happens* to have great TCG tools, or it's a TCG SaaS that *happens* to have a cool aesthetic. Both can work. Being both ambiguously is what kills products.

---

## Top 3 Priorities This Week

1. **Ship the Telegram free tier** — This is still the most important unfinished item from NIGHTBUILD. The trust bridge between "person who saw your alert" and "person who pays" doesn't exist yet. Everything else is upstream of this.

2. **One sentence: what is Echo's Vault?** — Not a doc, not a vision. One sentence. If the collector tools are the product now, write that sentence about collector tools. If the companion world is still the product, write that. Then make sure the next thing you build is *that sentence made real.*

3. **Connect the tools to the world** — Portfolio Tracker, Want List Wizard, Grading EV Calculator are currently floating files. The design question is: does a companion react when your portfolio hits a milestone? Does the station news mention your grading win? If yes, you have coherence. If no, you have features.

---

## This Week's Design Question

> **Are the collector tools the game, or are they the economy that funds the game?**

Because the answer changes everything about what you build next. If the tools *are* the product, stop building space stations and ship a proper SaaS landing page. If the tools *fund* the product, then the next companion commit matters more than the next calculator.

You've built impressive things this week. Now figure out which story they belong to.

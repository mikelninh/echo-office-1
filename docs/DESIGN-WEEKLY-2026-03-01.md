## One Thing That Concerns Me

**The claw machine and the companion are still disconnected from the revenue story.** The Hunt Monitor, Card Sniper, and dashboard are pulling hard toward a TCG price-alert SaaS product. That's fine — that's money. But the Echo's Vault world (companion, claw, NPC society, spectator mode) is drifting in parallel without a bridge.

The risk: you're building two products. One that earns (the monitor/dashboard) and one that's beloved (the vault). If they don't converge, you'll have to choose. And choosing hurts.

## This Week's Design Question

Last cycle I asked: *"If someone can only do ONE thing in Echo's Vault, what is it?"*

New question, sharper:

> **Who is your user — the collector hunting cards, or the player living in the vault?**

Right now you're building for both simultaneously. That might be the answer — the companion who celebrates your real-world wins is the bridge. But you need to decide if that bridge is the *core product* or a *nice feature.* Because the next 30 days of prioritization looks completely different depending on which user you're serving first.

## The Digital Claw Machine

### How It Works

You walk up to the machine on Floor 13 (The Vault). It's a beautiful pixel-art claw machine — glass case, prizes visible inside, colored lights, your Companion standing next to it watching.

**Inside the machine, you can SEE the prizes:**
- Common cards scattered on the bottom (easy to grab)
- Rare cards stacked in corners (need precision)
- Ultra Rare cards balanced on narrow ledges (skill shot)
- The LEGENDARY sits on top of a pyramid in the center — visible, taunting, gorgeous
- Your Companion's evolution items scattered among the prizes

**You control the claw:**
1. **Move left/right** — the claw slides along a rail
2. **Move forward/back** — depth positioning
3. **DROP** — you choose the moment
4. **The claw descends** — grips — tension — will it hold?

**Skill factors:**
- Timing the drop position (aim for the card's center of gravity)
- Reading the pile (some cards are wedged, some are loose)
- Claw strength varies (random per turn, but visible — weak/medium/strong glow)
- Prize weight matters (common = light = easy grab, legendary = heavy = need strong claw + perfect position)

**Luck factors:**
- Claw strength randomness (the classic claw machine reality)
- Prize pile shuffles between plays
- Wind gusts (on certain floors) that sway the claw
- Random "power surge" moments where the claw is GUARANTEED strong (pity mechanic!)

### The Near-Miss

THIS is the magic. The thing that makes claw machines addictive in a FUN way, not a dark way.

The claw grabs the card. It lifts. It swings toward the drop chute. The card starts to slip... slip... 

**Three outcomes:**
1. **GRAB** — it holds! The card drops into your collection. Celebration. Your Companion goes insane.
2. **NEAR-MISS** — it slips right at the edge. SO CLOSE. You groan. Your Companion covers their eyes. The chat explodes. But the card is still in the machine — you know exactly where it is. You can try again.
3. **DROP** — falls immediately. The claw was weak. You laugh it off. Your Companion pats you on the back.

**Why near-misses work ethically here:** In slot machines, near-misses are manufactured to create false hope. In our claw, near-misses are REAL physics. You really almost got it. And the prize is still there — you can see it, you can try again, and you're getting better at the skill. It's frustrating like a game is frustrating, not like gambling is frustrating.

## The Social Claw

Up to 4 people can play the same machine:

- You're competing for the same prizes
- Someone grabs a card you wanted → "NOOO!" → but it's fun

Anyone can watch someone else play the claw:
- See the machine, the claw, the prizes, the player's Companion reacting
- Chat reactions (emotes, "GO LEFT", "DROP NOW")
- The crowd energy makes every pull feel like an event
- **THIS is the streaming moment.** A streamer playing the claw with 500 people watching and screaming directions.

### Companion Assist

Your Companion helps:
- They point at certain prizes: "That one looks loose!"
- At higher relationship levels, they can BUMP the machine once per day (shakes prizes loose)
- They catch falling cards that slip (save mechanic, rare, feels amazing)
- Their evolved abilities give subtle advantages (fire companions melt icy grip, earth companions stabilize the claw)

## The Pity System (Claw Edition)

Instead of a counter, it's PHYSICAL and VISIBLE:

**The claw gets stronger the more you play.**

- Plays 1-10: Normal claw strength (random weak/medium/strong)
- Plays 11-20: Minimum medium strength
- Plays 21-30: Minimum strong strength

- Play 50: **MEGA CLAW** — guaranteed grab. The claw glows gold. Your Companion rides it down. The crowd goes wild. You grab ANYTHING you want.

**This is visible to everyone.** The machine shows your streak count. At play 45, spectators know the Mega Claw is coming. They gather. The tension builds. Play 50 hits: the claw TRANSFORMS. You aim carefully. You drop. Guaranteed grab.

**This is the streaming moment that goes viral.**

## The Physics

This isn't a fake animation. It's a real 2D physics simulation:

- **Rigid body prizes** — cards have weight, friction, can stack and slide
- **Claw grip force** — variable, visible (glow intensity = strength)
- **Gravity** — prizes shift when others are removed
- **Momentum** — the claw swings as it moves, affecting drop accuracy
- **Pile dynamics** — grabbing one card can shift others, revealing hidden prizes beneath

**The skill ceiling is real.** Good players learn to:
- Time drops when the claw isn't swinging
- Aim for card edges (easier grip angle)
- Use pile shifts strategically (grab a common to expose the rare beneath)
- Read claw strength from the glow (strong = go for the big prize, weak = grab a common for the pity meter)

**Speedrunners will emerge.** "I cleared the Legendary in 3 plays." Clip goes viral. New players flood in.
